## What you need to know

- Loom is the best choice for fast async communication with built-in sharing, viewer analytics, and commenting. It is the tool most enterprise teams already accept.
- Screen Studio produces the best visual output for product demos and is the right choice for polished sales collateral on Mac.
- Tella is the best choice for presenter-forward content where camera presence and presentation polish matter as much as the screen content.
- Async video is a higher-bandwidth communication medium than text for complex information that benefits from visual demonstration, but it is not a substitute for synchronous calls in high-stakes sales conversations.
- Recording hygiene matters as much as tool choice: a well-structured three-minute async video with a clear agenda outperforms a ten-minute unscripted recording in any tool.

## The core argument

The case for async video in founder communication is that it solves the problem that makes text communication fail for complex product demonstrations, nuanced investor updates, and feedback on design decisions. Text cannot convey tone reliably, cannot demonstrate a product interaction, and cannot efficiently communicate context that requires multiple paragraphs and screenshots to approximate what a two-minute video shows directly. A Loom that shows "here is the new onboarding flow, here is what changed, and here is why" takes four minutes to record and four minutes to watch. The equivalent email takes twenty minutes to write and produces a worse outcome because the reader has to imagine what they would have seen.

The tool choice depends on the use case. Loom dominates team communication and customer success because the sharing and viewing infrastructure is what enables async communication as a workflow, not just as a file. When you share a Loom, you know who watched it, when they watched it, and how long. You can see at which point in the recording viewers dropped off. The recipient can comment at a specific timestamp. These features turn a video recording into an async conversation. For a founder sending an investor update, a product walkthrough to a sales prospect, or a team announcement about a decision, the viewing data that Loom provides is often more valuable than the recording quality difference between Loom and Screen Studio.

Screen Studio earns its recommendation for outbound-facing product demos where visual quality creates a perception of product quality. The automatic zoom and keyboard visualization features produce recordings that look professionally edited with no post-processing. A cold outreach video that shows the product feature the prospect cares about, with clean transitions and on-screen keyboard shortcuts highlighted, converts better than the equivalent Loom recording. For founders who regularly record product demos to send to prospects, the one-time Screen Studio purchase pays back within the first few deals where better demo quality contributed to conversion.

## Common mistakes

1. **Recording without a clear structure.** A video that starts with "so I wanted to share some updates" and runs for twelve minutes is an imposition on the viewer's time. Structure every recording with a brief agenda: what you are showing, why it matters, and what you want the viewer to do after watching.

2. **Not trimming the beginning and end of recordings.** Most recorders capture the moment before the presenter is ready to start and the moment after they have finished. Trimming two to three seconds from each end produces a more professional result. All three tools have basic trimming features.

3. **Using Loom for polished outbound demos.** Loom's default recording quality and lack of camera customization make it less suitable for externally-facing sales content. Use Screen Studio or Tella for demos that represent the product to prospects.

4. **Not including a transcript or summary.** For team communication videos that contain important decisions or context, include the key points as text in the video description or as a linked document. Viewers who cannot watch the video immediately can get the essential information from the text.

5. **Sending long videos to investors without a written summary.** An investor who receives a twelve-minute Loom without context does not know whether the content is urgent or informational. Send async investor updates with a one-paragraph written summary followed by the video for detail. The summary ensures the message lands even for investors who skim.

## Where to start

1. **Choose one tool for internal team communication.** Loom is the default recommendation. Set up a shared Loom workspace for the team, establish the convention for when a video is the right communication format (demos, walkthroughs, feedback on designs) versus when text is sufficient.

2. **Record one product demo with Screen Studio.** Use the free trial for a prospect-facing product walkthrough. Compare the output to a Loom recording of the same content. Evaluate whether the visual quality difference justifies the purchase for your sales use case.

3. **Establish a three-minute maximum for internal updates.** A recording discipline that caps most updates at three minutes produces better, more structured content than open-ended recording. The constraint forces preparation and editing.
